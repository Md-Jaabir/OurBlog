BDMO Junior category problem with solution

Question:

Find the summation of the first 100 terms of the series 1+2+5+6+9+10+13+14+...

Solution:

To find the sum of the given series, let's first examine the pattern. The difference between the first two terms is 1 and the difference between the second and third term is 3. The same pattern continues.

Now, the difference between two consecutive odd terms and two consecutive even terms is 4. We can thus divide the given series into two arithmetic series:


First series, S1: 1+5+9+13+...

Second series, S2: 2+6+10+14+...

Now, let's use the formula for the sum of the first n terms of an arithmetic series:

For the first series, the number of terms, n=50 (since it includes all odd terms), the first term, a=1, and the common difference, d=4. Using the formula, we get:

S1 = (n/2) x {2a + (n-1)d}

= (50/2) x {2x1 + (50-1)x4}

= 25 x (2+196)

4950


For the second series, the number of terms, n=50 (since it includes all even terms), the first term, a=2, and the common difference, d=4. Using the formula, we get:

S2 = (n/2) x {2a + (n-1)d}

(50/2) x {2x2 + (50-1)x4}

= 25 x (4+196)

= 5000


Finally, the sum of the first 100 terms of the given series is the sum of S1 and S2:

Sum = S1 + S2

= 4950 + 5000

= 9950


Therefore, the sum of the first 100 terms of the given series is 9950.

In conclusion, we have shown how to find the summation of the first 100 terms of the series 1+2+5+6+9+10+13+14+... by dividing it into two arithmetic series based on the parity of the terms. By using the formula for the sum of an arithmetic series, we were able to calculate the sum of the first 50 odd terms and the first 50 even terms separately. Adding the two sums gave us the answer of 9950 for the first 100 terms of the series. This problem demonstrates the usefulness of recognizing patterns and applying mathematical formulas to find solutions.
